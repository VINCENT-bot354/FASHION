Finding Your Perfect Size

Knowing your measurements is the key to finding clothing that fits perfectly. Here's our guide to getting it right:

For accurate measurements, use a soft measuring tape and wear lightweight clothes or measure directly against your skin.

For Tops and Dresses:
  • Bust/Chest: Measure around the fullest part of your bust/chest, keeping the tape parallel to the floor.
  • Waist: Measure around the narrowest part of your natural waist, usually just above the belly button.
  • Hips: Measure around the fullest part of your hips, about 8 inches below your waist.
For Bottoms:
  • Waist: Measure around your natural waistline.
  • Hips: Measure around the fullest part of your hips.
  • Inseam: Measure from the crotch down to the ankle or desired length.

If you're between sizes or have experienced issues with clothing fit in the past, here are some helpful tips:

Between Sizes?
  • For looser styles (like loose tees, casual dresses), choose the smaller size.
  • For fitted styles, choose the larger size and consider alterations if needed.
  • For bottoms, prioritize waist fit—waistbands can be altered more easily than legs or seat.
Different Body Proportions?
  • Pear-shaped: Size based on your hip measurement for bottoms and bust for tops.
  • Apple-shaped: Size based on your waist measurement and consider flowy tops or A-line dresses.
  • Hourglass: Consider stretch materials or styles with adjustable features to accommodate both bust and waist.

Remember, if you need any specific advice on sizing for a particular product, please contact us via WhatsApp—we're happy to help!

Perfume Selection Tips

Finding your signature scent is a personal journey. Here's our guide to help you discover the perfect fragrance:

Fragrances are grouped into families based on their dominant characteristics. Understanding these can help you identify what you like:

Main Fragrance Families:
  • Floral: Based on flower scents like rose, jasmine, and lily. Romantic, feminine, and versatile.
  • Oriental: Warm, sweet, and spicy with notes like vanilla, amber, and exotic spices. Rich and sensual.
  • Woody: Based on warm woods like sandalwood, cedar, and vetiver. Elegant and grounding.
  • Fresh: Clean, light scents including citrus, aquatic, and green notes. Refreshing and invigorating.

If you already have a favorite perfume, identifying its family can help you discover similar scents you might enjoy.

Perfumes come in different concentrations, which affects their strength and longevity:

  • Parfum/Extrait: 20-30% fragrance concentration. Lasts 6-8 hours or more. The most potent and long-lasting.
  • Eau de Parfum (EDP): 15-20% concentration. Lasts 4-5 hours. Good balance of strength and value.
  • Eau de Toilette (EDT): 5-15% concentration. Lasts 2-3 hours. Lighter and more affordable.
  • Eau de Cologne (EDC): 2-4% concentration. Lasts 1-2 hours. Very light and refreshing.

Consider your needs when choosing a concentration. For everyday wear, EDT might be sufficient, while special occasions might call for EDP or Parfum.

Here are some tips for testing and selecting the right perfume:

Testing Tips:
  • Test perfume on your skin, not just on paper, as your body chemistry affects how a fragrance develops.
  • Apply to pulse points (wrists, neck, behind ears) where body heat will activate the scent.
  • Try only 1-2 fragrances at a time to avoid olfactory fatigue.
  • Give the perfume time to develop—the initial scent (top notes) will change after 10-15 minutes as the heart and base notes emerge.
Choosing Based on Occasion:
  • Daytime/Office: Lighter scents, such as fresh or light florals.
  • Evening/Formal: Richer scents like orientals or woody fragrances.
  • Seasons: Lighter, citrusy scents for summer; warmer, spicier scents for winter.

Remember, the best perfume is one that makes you feel confident and comfortable. Your personal preference is the most important factor!

Clothing Care Guide

Proper care will keep your garments looking beautiful for longer. Here are our expert tips:

Here are some universal clothing care tips:

Washing:
  • Always check the care label before washing.
  • Sort clothes by color and fabric type.
  • Turn garments inside out to protect prints and reduce friction.
  • Use cold water when possible to preserve colors and fabrics.
  • Consider hand washing or using mesh bags for delicate items.
Drying:
  • Air-dry delicate fabrics and items prone to shrinking.
  • Hang shirts and dresses to prevent wrinkles.
  • Lay knitwear flat to dry to prevent stretching.
  • When using a dryer, use the lowest heat setting necessary.
Storage:
  • Ensure clothes are completely dry before storing.
  • Hang structured garments like blazers and dresses.
  • Fold knitwear and t-shirts to avoid stretching.
  • Store seasonal items in breathable garment bags or cotton containers.

Different fabrics require specific care approaches:

Cotton:
  • Machine washable, but may shrink in hot water.
  • Can be ironed at high temperatures if needed.
  • Tends to wrinkle, so remove promptly from dryer.
Polyester:
  • Machine washable and wrinkle-resistant.
  • Air dry or tumble dry on low heat.
  • If ironing is needed, use low temperature.
Silk:
  • Dry clean or gentle hand wash with mild detergent.
  • Do not wring or twist; blot with towel and lay flat to dry.
  • Iron on low heat while slightly damp, using a pressing cloth.
Wool:
  • Hand wash with wool-specific detergent or dry clean.
  • Do not hang to dry; lay flat to preserve shape.
  • Store folded with cedar blocks to protect from moths.
Denim:
  • Wash jeans inside out in cold water to preserve color.
  • Wash less frequently to maintain fit and color.
  • Air dry when possible to prevent shrinking.

Quick action is key when dealing with stains. Here's how to handle common stains:

General Stain Tips:
  • Act quickly—fresh stains are easier to remove.
  • Blot, don't rub, to avoid spreading the stain or damaging fabric.
  • Test stain removers on an inconspicuous area first.
  • Work from the outside of the stain inward.
Common Stains:
  • Coffee/Tea: Rinse with cold water from the back of the stain, then treat with liquid detergent before washing.
  • Oil/Grease: Sprinkle with cornstarch or baby powder to absorb oil, then brush off and treat with dish soap before washing.
  • Red Wine: Blot with a paper towel, then cover with salt or baking soda. Rinse with cold water and wash normally.
  • Makeup: Use makeup remover or a small amount of dish soap, then wash according to garment instructions.
  • Blood: Soak in cold water (never hot), then treat with hydrogen peroxide (if safe for fabric) before washing.

For stubborn or delicate fabric stains, consider professional cleaning to avoid damage.
